### **PATTERN 1: Energy Leak at Lead Knee**

**Data Signatures:**
- Lead knee angle at contact: **< 140Â°** (flexed/bent)

**What This Means:**
- Ground force IS being generated (good)
- But energy is ABSORBED at the knee (leak)
- Hip contribution is weak despite strong legs
- Shoulders over-rotate to compensate
- Result: Bat drag, no whip, ground balls

**Root Cause:**
- **Coordination issue**, NOT strength issue
- Lead knee flexing instead of extending/stiffening
- 4-bar system not rigid (energy absorption vs transfer)

**Visual Cue (if video available):**

### **PATTERN 2: Weak Hip Contribution (Despite Good Timing)**

**Data Signatures:**
- Hip-shoulder timing gap: **Good** (e.g., 15-25ms)
- Hip angular momentum: **Low** (e.g., < 5.0)
- Hip deceleration: **Present** (hips DO slow down)
- Shoulder/Hip ratio: **High** (e.g., > 4.0x)
- Lead knee angle: **Flexed** at contact

**What This Means:**
- Timing sequence is correct (hips before shoulders)
- But MAGNITUDE is wrong (weak hip input)
- Shoulders forced to compensate for weak hips
- Has the deceleration mechanism but nothing to decelerate

**Root Cause:**
- Lead knee leak preventing energy from reaching hips
- Weak vertical ground force (vGRF < 1.0x bodyweight)
- Lack of hip lock (pelvic instability)

**Key Distinction:**
- This is NOT "rotate hips faster"
- This IS "get more energy TO the hips first"
