Why the ball is doing what it's doing

Eric, here's the truth: The ball is giving you instant feedback on every swing. Ground balls? That's the ball saying "you hit my top half." Roll-overs on breaking balls? That's the ball saying "your barrel crossed me too early."

Your mechanics aren't wrong because I say so. They're wrong because the ball says so. And the ball don't lie.

You're sweeping ACROSS instead of driving THROUGH. Think about it like this: If you throw a punch and pull your arm back halfway through, you lose all your power. Same thing here‚Äîyou're pulling your lead arm across instead of extending it through contact.

The fix? We need to teach that front arm to STRETCH FORWARD at impact. When you do that, everything changes: the barrel stays in the zone longer, you hit the ball on the sweet spot, and you create backspin line drives instead of topspin grounders.
